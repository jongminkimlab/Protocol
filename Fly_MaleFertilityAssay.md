

# Fly Male Fertility Assay (Single-Male × 3 Virgin Females)

Outline provided by Jongmin Kim (jk2938@cornell.edu), markdown produced by ChatGPT, and modified by Jongmin. <br>Last updated, Feb. 22, 2026

## Overview
This assay measures male fertility by quantifying the number of adult progeny (eclosed flies) produced from a single male crossed to three wild-type virgin females under standardized conditions. In addition to total progeny, **male and female progeny are counted separately** to detect potential sex-ratio distortion (e.g., meiotic drive) associated with chromatin factor dysfunction.

**Core design**
- Age males and virgin females **3 days** post-eclosion before mating
- Cross **1 male : 3 OregonR virgin females** per vial
- Allow mating/egg-laying for **5 days**
- Remove parents on day 5
- Count eclosed progeny **by sex**
- Score **≥10 vials per genotype** (more recommended if variability is high)

---

## Materials
### Flies
- Males of various genotypes (experimental)
- **OregonR virgin females** (wild-type, w1118 fine)  
 - Collect virgins within an appropriate window post-eclosion (typically within 6–8 hr; use your lab standard)

### Consumables
- **R (rich, brown) food vials** (consistent batch across genotypes if possible)

---

## Experimental Setup & Controls
- Use the **same rearing conditions** (temperature - 25C, humidity, density) for all genotypes.
- Use **age-matched parents**: both males and virgin females aged **3 days**.
- Use the **same food type** (R food) and similar vial fill level.
- Consider including a **reference control genotype** each run (e.g., OregonR male crossed to OregonR virgins).

---

## Timeline (Example)
- **Day -3 to 0**: Collect males and virgin females upon eclosion; isolate and age
- **Day 0**: Set up crosses (1 male + 3 virgin females per vial)
- **Day 5**: QC check + remove parents
- **Day 15–20** (typical at 25°C): Begin counting eclosed progeny daily until no new adults appear for ~2 consecutive days

(Exact emergence window depends on temperature and genotype.)

## Procedure

### 1) Collect and age flies (3 days)
1. Collect newly eclosed males of each genotype.
2. Place **single males** into individual vials (or individual tubes/vials per male), and **age in isolation for 3 days**.
3. Collect **OregonR virgin females** and place into vials (group-housed is fine) and **age for 3 days**.

**Notes**
- Avoid overcrowding during the 3-day aging period.

---

### 2) Set up mating vials (Day 0)
1. Label fresh **R food vials** with:
 - Genotype (male)
 - Date set
 - Vial # (e.g., G1–G12)
 - Your initials
2. Anesthetize briefly and add:
 - **1 experimental male** (3 days old)
 - **3 OregonR virgin females** (3 days old)
3. Plug vials securely and place all vials in the same incubator location if possible (or randomize positions).

**Replication**
- Set up **≥10 vials per genotype** (more if fertility is expected to be low or variable).

---

### 3) Remove parents + QC rule (Day 5)
1. On **Day 5** after setup, inspect each vial:
 - Confirm that **all four parents (1 male + 3 females) are alive**.
2. **Discard the vial from analysis if any of the four parents are missing or dead** at Day 5.
3. For valid vials, remove all parents from the vial on Day 5 (discard or save as needed).

**Why this QC rule?**
- Missing/dead parents can strongly confound progeny counts (reduced egg-laying, remating failure, stress effects).

---

### 4) Incubate until progeny eclose
1. Keep vials undisturbed until adults begin to eclose.
2. Once eclosion starts, check vials regularly (daily if possible) until counts plateau.

**Optional handling**
- If vials become too crowded, you may transfer newly eclosed adults to a “counting” vial, but keep careful records and do not lose flies.

---

### 5) Count progeny (by sex)
1. Count the number of **eclosed adult progeny** from each vial.
2. **Record males and females separately**:
 - Male progeny count
 - Female progeny count
 - Total progeny = males + females

**Stopping rule**
- Stop counting when **no new adults appear for ~2 consecutive days** (or your lab’s standard endpoint).

## Data Recording (Recommended Template)
Record one row per vial:

| Date Set | Genotype (Male) | Vial ID | # Parents alive at Day 5? (Y/N) | Day Parents Removed | Male Progeny | Female Progeny | Total Progeny | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|

**Notes column examples**
- Escapers during setup
- Mold/food desiccation
- Unusual development timing
- Any handling anomalies

---

## Notes & Troubleshooting
- **Low progeny across all genotypes**: check food quality, temperature, humidity, and CO₂ exposure during setup.
- **High variability**: increase vial number; ensure consistent parental age and handling.
- **Skewed sex ratios**: double-check sexing accuracy; confirm with replicate experiments.
